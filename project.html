  • Smart Home Security

    This project focuses on enhancing the security of smart homes by addressing the vulnerabilities found in IoT-based devices such as smart locks, cameras, and sensors. Our team researched how weak authentication, outdated firmware, and poor data privacy can expose users to risks, and proposed effective protection measures like strong Wi-Fi encryption, regular software updates, and two-factor authentication. The project also included an analysis of real-world attacks, such as the Mirai Botnet, to understand the importance of secure configurations in connected environments. It was developed as part of a group presentation for the "Smart Home Security" topic in our cybersecurity fundmenals course.
